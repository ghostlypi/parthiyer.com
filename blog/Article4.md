# Why Waymos are bad drivers

<span style="color: grey;">Published: 2025-11-27</span>

<details>
<summary>Description</summary>
I finally rode in a Waymo and learned why it is a bad driver. The speed wasn't the problem.
</details>

So I finally managed to get myself into a Waymo on the way back home from the Airport. The cost happened to match that of
a rideshare, so I figured why not finally try out a car fully driving itself without human supervision. I sat back and watched 
as the ride started out smoothly, however, this was not long to last.

The first major obstacle along the way was a right lane closure where multiple vehicles were trying to merge into the left lane.
While a human driver would try and allow a car from the right lane through between them and the car ahead, the Waymo had no such
awareness, closely following the car in front of it rather than doing the polite thing and letting another motorist through.
This experience would be characteristic of how the rest of the ride would go.

The Waymo attempted to frequently shift lanes whenever the cars in front of it would slow down, often failing to do so,
even when there was ample room to switch lanes as a result of human driver kindness. The car didn't think there was sufficient
space and would stay in the lane and not switch, leaving human drivers frustrated, often giving up and speeding past.
Likewise, the Waymo would not give way to other drivers either, leading to a ride experience which felt unaware of other vehicles.

On top of the social faux pas, the Waymo had few understanding fails, drifting between lanes a couple of times, detecting a
hand-held stop sign in a construction zone on the other side of the road, and even losing the lane, ending with a sudden correction.
While the run of the mill errors were funny to watch, they are the easiest to correct and can be simply fixed by more training.
The unwritten social norms of driving will be much harder to teach to a model as they are uncodified, and more inconsistent.

The best way to describe the driving style of a Waymo is that of a driver who strictly follows the rules, without any consideration
for the specifics of its environment, or an understanding of inter-human interactions. There is an unwritten set of rules of the road,
which human drivers accomplish instinctively, but which automated drivers are unable to pick up on yet. Going the speed limit, whilst annoying, 
is not so much a flaw as the inability to behaviorally negotiate with other drivers and get out of their way.
