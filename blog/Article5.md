# The biggest challenge to the AI bubble

<span style="color: grey;">Published: 2025-12-08</span>

<details>
<summary>Description</summary>
The memory industry is putting it's finger on the scale.
</details>

The AI industry is in trouble. Companies have been scaling their hardware exponentially for linear increments in performance, 
however access to such hardware may be limited in the future. All three major manufacturerers (Micron, SK Hynix, and Samsung)
have opted to **not** increase their Memory Production, putting DRAM and HBM production in direct conflict, sending Prices 
to the moon.

As of the date of publishing, the commodities price of DRAM is an average of $27 per 8 GB chip of DDR5 
(the current standard). This is a 100% month on month increase in one essential resource for AI servers [1]. While the GPUs themselves
use HBM 3 (High Bandwidth Memory), the servers still require copious amounts of DDR5 to store models in transit between non-volatile storage
and GPU training, requiring enough space to fit the while model in memory. As a result of this, the pricing of this component will no longer
align with existing models, and will ultimately affect the price scaling for new data centers.

Even if we see manufacturers move to expand capacity today, scaling will be significantly constrained for the next 5 years 
as new advanced memory fabrication plants (fabs) come online, in a similar time frame to semiconductor fabs. This is going to be
a big hurdle for the AI industry in the coming years, as any hamper to growth may decrease investor confidence, making the industry more 
vulnerable to collapse.
